Super Smash Bros. Melee

The game is neat and it having a competitive scene is well deserved. Unfortunately I'm not too good at the game. I could be getting better but don't have time to throw around and don't have an ethernet cable to play online. You can find me getting last place along with 1/4 of other players at a high profile tournament here.

I've had the game since forever, but I picked up the game in around freshman year. I knew there was a competitive scene, but I didn't play with intent to compete, but to explore. This was because I picked Yoshi randomly, and accidentally discovered Double Jump Canceling, which was pretty cool. This random choice is why I still play Yoshi, although he is not a perfect character. I found my neighbor also played, and my brother set me up to fight him. I was destroyed, if I recall correctly. This started my foray into competition.

I'd like to say I'm good at the game, but I can't really say much for results. I don't blame my character. I get stomped by people at my school who play. I go slightly less than even with my brother. It all comes down to experience, and I don't get much playing the AI. Here's hoping I get better in college.

Tetris (and Puyo Puyo)

I really didn't go into Tetris expecting competitive gameplay, but it emerged somehow. In 2017, I saw a reddit post (in /r/smashbros actually) about Tetris. It was pretty informative and even had a meme video (boom tetris for jeff). I didn't think much about it until I was bored in class with a Chromebook. I ended up doing a lot of sprints in jstris, the best Tetris fan client. Discovering more depth other than speed, I learned most spins for all the pieces offhandedly. I also roped my friend group and my brother into Tetris somehow.

I also grew interested in Puyo Puyo due to Puyo Puyo Tetris videos. I think it was this game that drew my friends and brother.

Here are some (poor) measurements of skill. I have a 3.5k elo in Puyo Puyo Tetris, playing Swap and Versus (Tetris). I have an 89 second 40-Line Sprint, with obvious mistakes other than hesitation. I can do most spins for all pieces, amd I can t-spin double and triple freehandedly. I have serviceable finesse with fast DAS. I have done an 8 chain in single player Puyo. Not impressive, but also still enough to be better than most people.
